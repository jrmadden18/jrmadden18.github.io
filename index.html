Jocelyn's Dance Page

Dance has many physical and mental benefits. Being involved in dance in any capacity, whether by attending professional performances, taking studio classes, or becoming a professional dancer is enjoyable and impactful. I have taken dance classes for the past 5 years and have benefited greatly from them. I'm based in Upstate, South Carolina in the Spartanburg and Inman areas.

the universal importance of dance

Dance has many benefits that make dancers well-rounded that I have noticed throughout my 5 years dancing. I started dancing ballet when I was 12. I have danced a variety of additional styles including Irish step-dancing, tap, Broadway jazz, and hip-hop.  Dance has many of the benefits of being on a sports team in addition to the benefits from being in a choir or orchestra. For example, I have gained confidence, physical strength, in addition to rhythm and tempo skills. I also enjoy attending ballets and musicals. Getting involved in dance in any capacity is beneficial to everyone.

attending professional performances

If you want to become a professional dancer, attending professional performances in inspiring to see those in your career field perform amazing technical feats in emotional, thought-provoking numbers. Even if you don't plan to become a dancer, watching full-length ballets is an amazing experience, like watching a musical, play, or opera but with its own unique appeal. If you live in the Upstate, Ballet Spartanburg is our only local professional ballet company. They have performed many wonderful shows over the years, from famous ballets like "The Nutcracker" and "Coppelia," to company showcases and original pieces. Learn more on Ballet Spartanburg's website. 

taking studio classes

Attending classes at a dance studio grows your dance ability and gives you a sense of community. It is enjoyable to learn from and perform with your peers. Personally, I attend Emmanuel Arts, a small, recreational dance studio in Inman, South Carolina. Emmanuel Arts is dedicated to providing affordable access to dance, offering a variety of inexpensive classes including ballet, pointe, Broadway jazz, tap, Irish step-dancing, acrobatics, and Zumba. Other arts like theatre and martial arts (Korean Tang Soo Do) are also offered. Learn more on Emmanuel Arts Facebook. Different dance studios are better fits for different students. Another Inman studio, Starmakers, is a larger, competition dance company that offers classes and competition teams for contemporary, lyrical, and hip-hop. Their sister school Realta is a competition Irish step-dancing school. Learn more on the Starmakers website.

physical and mental health benefits

There are a multitude of mental and physical benefits to taking dance classes, whether recreationally or competitively. The combination of athletic activity in an art form provides unique benefits to athletic activity. Dancers learn skills like teamwork, keeping rhythm, coordination, and balance. Students work individually or together to perform choreography, creating an art piece. For some, this can be more rewarding than winning a ball game. The combination of art and athletics benefits both the brain and the body.
